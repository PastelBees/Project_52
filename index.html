Camel

  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can’t eat
  • Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms

Chameleon

  • Chameleons are reptiles that are part of the iguana suborder
  • Changing skin color is an important part of communication among chameleons
  • Most chameleons have a prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches

Pigeon

  • Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals
  • Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities
  • Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities

Panda

  • Pandas' favorite food is bamboo shoots
  • An adult panda weight more than 45 kilos (100 pounds), and can be about 1.5 meters (5 feet) long!
  • Pandas have a 6th "thumb" on the heels of their fore-paws

Cicada

  • The loud whirring or buzzing sound you hear is an all-male cicada chorus
  • Most have red-orange eyes; but occasionally cicadas have blue, white or grayish eyes
  • They improve lawns by digging tunnels that allow air into the soil

Cat

  • Cats walk like camels and giraffes
  • A cat was the Mayor of an Alaskan town for 20 years
  • Cats spend 70% of their lives sleeping

Caterpillar

  • Caterpillars don’t have teeth
  • Caterpillars drool out silk for a cocoon
  • Caterpillars only have six true legs

Giraffe

  • Giraffes are the tallest mammals on Earth. Their legs alone are taller than many humans—about 6 feet
  • Giraffes spend most of their lives standing up; they even sleep and give birth standing up
  • They can run as fast as 35 miles an hour over short distances, or cruise at 10 mph over longer distances
